Dr. Vaishakh Kottila Veedu

Postdoctoral Researcher | Computational Solid Mechanics | Experimenal mechanics

I am a postdoctoral researcher in computational solid mechanics with a focus on damage and fracture modeling of advanced materials. My work integrates continuum theory, experimental observations, and finite element methods to develop predictive, physics-informed frameworks for heterogeneous materials, including fiber-reinforced composites, additively manufactured metals, and multi-material systems.

Research Interests

  • Damage & Fracture Mechanics (GTN, Nonlocal / Gradient-Enhanced Models)
  • Finite Deformation Plasticity & Multiscale Constitutive Modeling
  • Computational Mechanics of Composites and AM Materials

Selected Projects

  • Nonlocal GTN Framework: Thermodynamically consistent gradient-enhanced ductile damage models for mesh-independent fracture prediction.
  • Fracture in Additively Manufactured Materials: Phase-field and continuum damage approaches for heterogeneous microstructures.
